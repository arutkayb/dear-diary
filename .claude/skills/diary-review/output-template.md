# Reflection Output Template

The reflection file should follow this structure. Each section description explains what to put there and how to derive it from the diary data and reflection conversation.

---

```markdown
# Daily Reflection — {YYYY-MM-DD}

## Day Overview

**{N} sessions across {N} projects · {N} messages · ~{N}k tokens**

{1-2 sentences summarizing the day's character: what was the dominant theme, energy, or focus? Derive from session distribution and project types — e.g., "Heavy debugging and release day for AdvancedSCalendar, bookended by tooling improvements." Do not list every project here.}

---

## What I Worked On

{Bulleted list grouped by project or theme, not by session. Each bullet is 1-2 lines: what was done, not what was discussed. Use active past tense ("shipped", "fixed", "built", "investigated"). Order by significance, not time. 4-8 bullets for a typical day.}

- **{Project or Theme}** — {what was done}
- ...

---

## Reflections

### Wins
{2-4 bullets. Concrete accomplishments the user is proud of, or that unblocked something. In auto mode: infer from language in conversations (e.g., "pushed to prod", "tests pass", "finally figured out"). In reflection mode: use what the user said. Short and specific.}

### Learnings
{2-4 bullets. Things the user understood more deeply, discovered, or would apply again. In auto mode: look for moments of realization or design decisions in conversations. In reflection mode: pull directly from answers. Frame as insights, not events.}

### Challenges
{1-3 bullets. Friction points, blockers, or places where progress was slower than expected. Not complaints — just honest acknowledgment. In auto mode: look for long debug sessions, multiple retries, or rollbacks. In reflection mode: use what the user shared.}

### Patterns
{1-3 bullets. Recurring behaviors, workflow observations, or meta-observations about how the day went. E.g., "Context-switched 5 times across projects — only one deep-focus block." These are the "zoom out" observations. In reflection mode: may come from the user's own framing. In auto mode: derive from session timing and project spread.}

---

## Key Takeaways

{3-5 distilled points. Each is a standalone, quotable insight from the day — something a reader could extract and apply. Write as declarative statements, not bullets with labels. These are the "if you read nothing else" section.}

1. {Takeaway}
2. {Takeaway}
3. {Takeaway}

---

## Seeds for Content

{2-3 potential blog post or LinkedIn angles derived from today's work. Each is one sentence: the hook or the angle, not a full outline. These are starting points for Stage 3 (content-creator). Prioritize angles that would resonate with other developers or indie builders.}

- {Angle 1}
- {Angle 2}
- {Angle 3}
```
